## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mamaearth's unit economics have already breached the critical threshold. Despite revenue growing to ₹2,066 crore in FY25, the LTV:CAC ratio stands at **0.91x** — well below the 3x minimum for sustainable acquisition economics. Operating profit margin has simultaneously compressed from **7.1% (FY24) to 3.3% (FY25)**, a 380bps deterioration in a single year. The brand is acquiring customers at a cost that its lifetime value does not recover. boAt presents a contrasting picture: LTV:CAC at **4.65x**, EBITDA turning positive at **4.64%** after two loss years. But with an IPO pending and margins this thin, sustained CAC inflation will erode that buffer within two years. Both brands face the same structural risk — paid acquisition costs inflating faster than lifetime value growth — at very different starting positions.

---

## KEY RISK: THE METRIC MOST LIKELY TO TRIGGER A BREAKDOWN

**Primary Risk: Mamaearth's LTV:CAC is already below the sustainability threshold**

At 0.91x LTV:CAC, Mamaearth is generating ₹0.91 in lifetime gross profit for every ₹1 spent acquiring a customer. This is not a forward projection risk — it is the current state. The contributing factors are structural: CAC of ₹2,943 per new customer against an LTV of only ₹2,677, driven by a combination of high marketing intensity (29.9% of revenue) and insufficient AOV × frequency × lifespan to recover it.

Under the bear case (CAC +30% YoY, industry-documented 2022–2024 rate), the ratio deteriorates further to **0.49x by FY27** — the acquisition economics become irreversible without intervention.

| Brand | FY25 LTV:CAC | Bear Case FY27 | Breach Status |
|-------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| Mamaearth | **0.91x** | 0.49x | ⚠️ Already breached |
| boAt | 4.65x | 2.49x | ⚠️ Breach in FY27 (bear) |

**Secondary Risk (boAt-specific):** boAt's LTV:CAC is healthy today, but the bear case projects a breach by FY27. At 4.64% EBITDA pre-IPO, any post-listing cost increase — compliance overhead, investor relations, public company infrastructure — applied to flat revenue will erase profitability within one quarter. The IPO narrative depends on sustaining the FY25 cost discipline.

## RECOMMENDED INTERVENTIONS (Ranked by ROI)

### Mamaearth — Priority: Immediate

Given that Mamaearth is already below the 3x threshold, interventions must be evaluated on their ability to move the ratio toward 3x, not merely maintain it. All three levers are needed in combination; the ranking reflects highest revenue impact per rupee invested.

| Rank | Intervention | Investment | LTV:CAC Movement | Revenue Impact | ROI |
|------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|-----|
| 1 | AOV Increase (Bundling/Upsell) | ₹3 Cr | 0.91x → 1.02x (+0.11x) | ₹248 Cr | 80x |
| 2 | CAC Reduction (Paid→Organic Shift) | ₹0 Cr (reallocation) | 0.91x → 1.00x (+0.09x) | ₹103 Cr | — |
| 3 | Retention Improvement (CRM/Loyalty) | ₹31 Cr | 0.91x → 1.16x (+0.25x) | ₹33 Cr | 1.1x |

**Critical note:** No single intervention moves Mamaearth above 3x. These three in combination move the ratio to approximately 1.25x — meaningfully better, but still well below threshold. The root issue is CAC of ₹2,943 against a business model with an AOV of ₹581 and 2.7 orders per year. **The structural fix is AOV expansion, not acquisition efficiency.** A 12% AOV lift delivers ₹248 Cr revenue impact on ₹3 Cr investment — that ROI (80x) is the clearest lever available.

### boAt — Priority: Protect and Extend

boAt does not have an acute unit economics problem today. The priority is protecting the 4.65x ratio against CAC inflation ahead of IPO.

| Rank | Intervention | Investment | LTV:CAC Movement | Revenue Impact | ROI |
|------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|-----|
| 1 | AOV Increase (Premium Wearables Tier) | ₹15 Cr | 4.65x → 5.21x (+0.56x) | ₹368 Cr | 24x |
| 2 | CAC Reduction (Paid→Organic Shift) | ₹0 Cr (reallocation) | 4.65x → 5.12x (+0.46x) | ₹154 Cr | — |
| 3 | Retention Improvement (CRM/Loyalty) | ₹46 Cr | 4.65x → 5.52x (+0.86x) | ₹182 Cr | 3.9x |

**Recommendation:** The wearables segment is already 37% of revenue and growing. A premium tier within wearables — leveraging the category's higher ASP — delivers the highest revenue impact (₹368 Cr) at the lowest relative investment (₹15 Cr). This also strengthens the IPO narrative by demonstrating a clear premium-product growth path with improving unit economics, not just cost discipline.

| Scenario | Assumption | Mamaearth FY28 LTV:CAC | boAt FY28 LTV:CAC | Risk Level |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Bear | CAC +30% YoY, LTV -5% YoY | 0.35x | 1.82x | 🔴 Critical (both breach) |
| Base | CAC +15% YoY, LTV +2% YoY | 0.63x | 3.41x | 🟡 Mamaearth critical; boAt manageable |
| Bull | CAC -10% YoY, LTV +8% YoY | 1.68x | 7.20x | 🟡 Mamaearth recovering; boAt strong |

**The base case is not safe for Mamaearth.** Even under the base scenario — moderated CAC inflation with marginal LTV improvement — Mamaearth's LTV:CAC remains well below 3x through FY28. The bull case brings it to 1.68x, still below threshold. This confirms that Mamaearth's problem is not a CAC cycle issue that efficiency improvements can resolve — it is a structural AOV and LTV problem that requires product and commercial intervention.

**For boAt**, the base case holds above 3x. The bear case is the only scenario that triggers a breach (FY27). Sustaining marketing cost discipline and executing on the premium wearables opportunity keeps boAt in the healthy zone through IPO and beyond.
