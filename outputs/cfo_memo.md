## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mamaearth's unit economics are under measurable stress. Despite revenue growing to ₹2066 crore in FY25, operating profit margin has compressed from **7.1% (FY24) to 3.3% (FY25)** — a 380bps deterioration in a single year. The LTV:CAC ratio stands at **0.91x**, still above the 3x critical threshold but declining. boAt has executed a credible profitability turnaround — EBITDA at **4.64%** in FY25 after a loss year — but with an IPO pending and margins this thin, any CAC inflation cycle will remove the buffer. Both brands face the same structural risk: paid acquisition costs rising faster than customer lifetime value growth.

---

## KEY RISK: THE METRIC MOST LIKELY TO TRIGGER A BREAKDOWN

**Primary Risk: CAC inflation outpacing LTV expansion**

CAC has inflated at ~30% YoY across Indian D2C brands (2022–2024). If this rate holds:

| Brand | FY25 LTV:CAC | Bear Case FY27 LTV:CAC | Breach Year |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|-------------|
| Mamaearth | 0.91x | 0.49x | FY27 |
| boAt | 4.66x | 2.49x | FY27 |

The bear case is not a tail scenario — it reflects actual 2022–2024 market conditions. The question is whether either brand can generate enough efficiency improvement to neutralize it.

**Secondary Risk (boAt-specific):** At 4.64% EBITDA pre-IPO, any post-listing cost increase (compliance, public company overhead, investor relations) with flat revenue growth erases the profitability story within one quarter.

## RECOMMENDED INTERVENTIONS (Ranked by ROI)

### Mamaearth

| Rank | Intervention | Investment | LTV:CAC Improvement | Revenue Impact | ROI |
|------|-------------|-----------|---------------------|---------------|-----|
| 1 | Retention Improvement (CRM/Loyalty) | ₹31 Cr | +0.25x | ₹33 Cr | 1.1x |
| 2 | AOV Increase (Bundling/Upsell) | ₹10 Cr | +0.11x | ₹248 Cr | 24.0x |

**Recommendation:** The highest ROI move for Mamaearth is retention investment. Reducing annual churn by 10% through CRM and loyalty programming extends average customer lifespan by 8 months — adding ₹33 crore in incremental revenue at ₹31 crore investment. This is superior to pure CAC reduction because it improves LTV without touching the acquisition machine.

### boAt

**Recommendation:** boAt's FY25 efficiency gain came from marketing cost discipline — that's the playbook. Extending it via organic/creator channels reduces CAC further while preserving the IPO narrative. Premium wearables expand AOV; the segment already at 37% of revenue and growing.
